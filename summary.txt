On January 30, 2025, a stampede at the Maha Kumbh Mela religious event in Prayagraj, India resulted in at least 30 deaths and several injuries. The tragedy occurred as millions of Hindu devotees gathered to partake in ritualistic bathing in the Ganges River. Witnesses claim that police ignored pleas to open barricaded routes to manage the crowd, leading to panic. The incident has prompted investigations into the cause and the effectiveness of the crowd control measures. It has also sparked discussions about the need for improved safety protocols at large religious events in India. This event underscores the importance of crowd management and safety measures at mass gatherings, and the urgent need for better planning and preparedness to prevent such incidents in the future.
